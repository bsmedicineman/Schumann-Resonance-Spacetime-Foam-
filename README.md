# Schumann-Resonance-Spacetime-Foam

**Exploring the relationship between Earth's natural Schumann Resonances and spacetime foam / resonant scalar medium physics.**

This repository is dedicated to theoretical and experimental investigation of Schumann Resonances as potential macroscopic signatures or detection channels for spacetime foam and resonant scalar field dynamics.

---

## Overview

The **Schumann Resonances** are the natural extremely low frequency (ELF) electromagnetic standing waves that form in the spherical cavity between the Earth's surface and the lower ionosphere. Excited primarily by global lightning activity, they form a stable resonant "heartbeat" of the planet.

This project examines whether these global resonances — particularly the fundamental mode near **7.83 Hz** and its harmonics — can serve as sensitive probes for spacetime foam effects, resonant scalar medium interactions, or 4D navigation-related phenomena.

The repository will host:

- Theoretical models linking Schumann cavity physics to spacetime foam frameworks
- Data analysis pipelines for live and archived Schumann monitoring stations
- Hardware designs for precision ELF reception and resonance experiments
- Simulation tools for resonant scalar field interactions with planetary-scale cavities
- Documentation of experimental protocols and findings

## Scientific Background

The Schumann Resonances consist of a series of spectral peaks in the ELF band:

- **Fundamental mode**: ≈ 7.83 Hz
- **Harmonics**: ≈ 14.3 Hz, 20.8 Hz, 27.3 Hz, 33.8 Hz, and higher

These resonances are global, extremely stable under normal conditions, and can be perturbed by solar activity, ionospheric changes, and potentially other subtle influences. Their wavelengths are on the order of the Earth's circumference, making them one of the largest natural resonant systems on the planet.

This project treats the Earth-ionosphere cavity as a **planetary-scale resonant detector** and explores its potential coupling to underlying spacetime structure.
